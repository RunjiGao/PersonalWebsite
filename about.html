About Me

I am a Statistics M.A. student at Columbia University with a background in Economics, Data Science, and Psychology from New York University. My research interests center on how people make decisions, respond to social information, and interact with digital environments.

Across multiple research labs, I have worked on projects involving behavioral economics, social psychology, climate communication, and computational behavioral science. I am especially interested in combining experimental methods with statistical and computational tools to study human behavior.
